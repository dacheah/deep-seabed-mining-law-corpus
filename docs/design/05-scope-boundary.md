# 05 — Scope boundary (Deep Seabed Mining Law Corpus)

Grow **instrument by instrument**, not by sweep. Breadth is worthless if any record's provenance is
doubtful.

## In scope
The law governing mineral resources of **"the Area"** (the seabed and subsoil beyond national
jurisdiction) and the parallel US regime:

- **UNCLOS Part XI** (the Area) and the **1994 Agreement** implementing Part XI.
- **ISA instruments**: the three exploration regulations (polymetallic nodules; polymetallic
  sulphides; cobalt-rich ferromanganese crusts); the draft **Exploitation Regulations** ("the Mining
  Code"); associated **Legal and Technical Commission recommendations**; and ISA **decisions,
  standards and guidelines** (`ISBA/...`).
- The **2011 Seabed Disputes Chamber Advisory Opinion** (Case 17) — the key case law on
  sponsoring-state responsibility and liability.
- **Sponsoring-state national laws** enabling contractors under the ISA regime.
- The **US non-UNCLOS parallel track**: DSHMRA and its NOAA regulations (15 CFR 970/971), plus
  Outer Continental Shelf activity under OCSLA/BOEM — captured but clearly marked as the
  non-UNCLOS alternative (JC-001).

## Out of scope (until a dated decision)
- General law of the sea unrelated to the Area (navigation, EEZ resources, continental-shelf claims
  under Part VI, delimitation).
- Fisheries law (incl. the Central Arctic Ocean fisheries agreement — a *comparative reference* for the
  concept layer only, not authoritative-layer law).
- General environmental law and the Antarctic mining moratorium (Madrid Protocol) — comparative
  reference only.
- ISA **DeepData** scientific/environmental datasets — an adjacency, explicitly **not law**; never
  ingested into the authoritative layer.

## Borderline items
Anything on the law-of-the-sea / fisheries / environmental-law edge goes to `queue/candidates.md` for
a **recorded decision**, not straight into the corpus. Record every scope change as a dated note.
